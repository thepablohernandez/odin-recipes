Empanadas

Empanadas A.K.A. Argentina's best

From the sweet to the savory, there's no filling that isn't made better when it's wrapped in a delicate, flaky pastry.

Therein lies the magic of empanadas.

This popular Latin American food is renowned around the world as a delicious and filling main course. Today, we show you how to make one of Argentina's best recipes

Ready to make your mouth water? Read on for all you need to know

Ingredients

  • 3 Tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil, divided
  • 2 lb. ground beef (20% fat)
  • 2 medium onions, chopped
  • 2 small red bell peppers, seeded, chopped Kosher salt, freshly ground pepper
  • 3 Tbsp. ground cumin
  • 3 packages (12 each) Puff Pastry Dough for Turnovers/Empanadas
  • ½ cup pitted green olives (Picholine or Spanish), rinsed well, cut in half lengthwise, divided
  • ½ cup raisins

Steps

  1. Heat 2 Tbsp. oil in a large pot over high. Cook 2 lb. ground beef, breaking up with a spoon, until browned but not completely cooked through, 6–8 minutes.
  2. Reduce heat to medium and cook 2 medium onions, chopped, 2 small red bell peppers, seeded, chopped, and remaining 1 Tbsp. oil, stirring, until tender but not browned, 6–8 minutes
  3. Preheat oven to 375°
  4. Let 3 packages Puff Pastry Dough for Empanadas sit at room temperature 15 minutes to temper. Remove 6 rounds from package, keeping plastic divider underneath, and arrange on a work surface.
  5. Place 2 Tbsp. filling in the center of each round.
  6. Top with 2 olive halves. Brush water around half of outer edge of each round.
  7. Using plastic divider to help you, fold round over filling and pinch edges to seal.
  8. Using a fork, crimp edges. Remove plastic and transfer empanada to a parchment-lined sheet tray, spacing 1" apart.
  9. Bake empanadas, rotating tray halfway through, until golden brown and slightly darker around the edges, 25–35 minutes

Pro Tip

Do Ahead:Unbaked empanadas can be made 3 months ahead; freeze on sheet tray, then transfer to freezer bags and keep frozen.
